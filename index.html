Hi! My name is Harley. I'm the creator of Suite Knowledge, a place where small business owners, freelancers, and productivity enthusiasts can learn how to get the most value out of their Google Suite products.

Running a small business, freelance gig, or side hustle can require an upfront investment in tools you need to run your business. It's often tempting to subscribe to fancy services that claim to do something really well. Before you know it, those little subscriptions have added up. What if you could build those tools using just a Basic GSuite plan? Or, in some cases, your free personal Google account?

Here are some of the types of content you'll find on Suite Knowledge:

Projects

I write fun projects that demonstrate how useful tools (and even games!) can be built in Google Suite. The website is just getting started, but here are some of the projects I have in the pipeline:

  • a functional CRM;
  • a Gantt chart for project management;
  • and an extensible API backed by Google Sheets.

Projects walk you through every step, every mouse click, every line of code, so when you're done you have your own finished, working, useful tool!

And even if you don't think you'll have a use for these projects, they are a fun way for you to build your Suite skills and will open your eyes to what's possible with Google Suite.

Ultimate Guides

I'll also be publishing a series of Ultimate Guides. Each guide will attempt to build the ultimate something, such as:

  • The Ultimate Kanban Board
  • The Ultimate Time Tracker
  • The Ultimate Bullet Journal

You may have seen tutorials for these elsewhere online, but I find they usually do the bare minimum to get the job done, which goes against one of the core principles of Suite Knowledge: to push GSuite to its limits and explore unconventional uses for it. Hence why I'm calling these Ultimate Guides.

How-To's

These will be short blog posts that explain how to do some specific task.

Ebooks

I also have at least one ebook in the works. Ebooks will be available exclusively to patrons.

Online Community

My hope is to get enough traction here to justify setting up a Discourse server exclusively for patrons. You'll be able to access exclusive content, like ebooks and links to finished projects you can copy to your own GSuite account, and more.
